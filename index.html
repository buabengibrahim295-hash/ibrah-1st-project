Academic Journey

The history of Cape Coast Technical University (CCTU) began in 1984 as a Second Cycle Technical Institute. By 1992, it transitioned into Cape Coast Polytechnic under PNDC Law 321. A major milestone was reached in 2016 when it was converted into a full Technical University (Act 922).

The Computer Science course was developed to meet the growing technological demands of Ghana. It emphasizes Competency-Based Training (CBT), focusing on hardware, software engineering, and artificial intelligence, ensuring Level 200 students are ready for industrial innovation.
